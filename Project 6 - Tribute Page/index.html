President Robert Mugabe

~Former President of Zimbabwe~

One of the most revered "Founding Fathers of Zimbabwe"

About

Cde Robert Gabriel Mugabe was a Zimbabwean revolutionary and politician who served as Prime Minister of Zimbabwe (formerly Southern Rhodesia) from 1980 to 1987 and then as President from 1987 to 2017.

The history of the icon is celebrated in Zimbabwe on the 21st of February of every year.

This tribute highlights his early fife before he became President of the Republic of Zimbabwe.

Early life of Pres. R.G Mugabe.

  • 1924 - On February 21, Born on February 21, at Kutama Jesuit Mission, Zvimba
  • 1930 - Mugabe' had an argument with one of the Jesuits at school that resulted in the family being expelled from the mission village
  • 1934 - After losing his second brother from food poison, Robert's father left for Bulawayo to look for greener pastures
  • 1941 - Robert was offered a place on a teacher training course at Kutama College. Because of hardships, young Robert started teaching at his old school, earning 2pounds/month
  • 1944 - Robert graduated with a Diploma in teaching and left Kutama in 1945
  • 1949 - Obtained a scholarship to study at the University of Fort Hare, South Africa. It is here where he met other future leaders such as Julius Nyerere and Kenneth Kaunda
  • 1952 - Left the University with a BA in History and English literature and returned to Rhodesia
  • 1953 - Robert relocated to Highfield Govt School
  • 1954 - Relocated to Mambo Township Govt School in Gwelo
  • 1958 - Relocated to Ghana where he taught until 1960
  • 1960 - Mugabe returned to Southern Rhodesia and joined the National Democratic Party (NDP)
  • 1963 - The NDP was banned and Mugabe joined ZANU (Zimbabwe African National Union) led by Rev Ndabaningi Sithole
  • 1964 - Mugabe was arrested for subversive speech and spent the next 11yrs in a Salisbury prison
  • 1972 - While in prison, he was elected to lead ZANU
  • 1975 - Mugabe is released from prison. When Ian Smith, then Prime Minister of Rhodesia made no power sharing concessions to the black majority, Mugabe and other leaders began a guerrilla war against the Smith regime
  • 1980 - In February of this year, a cease-fire was negotiated which saw the first all-race election was held in Zimbabwe. Mugabe emerged the victor with 57 of 80 seats in the new parliament
